1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================

The University of Sri Jayewardenepura was established by the Democratic Socialist
republic of Sri Lanka under the provisions of Universities Act No 16 of 1978. It is an
autonomous university established under the provisions of this Act and the following
subsequent amending Acts.

Historical background:
The present University of Sri Jayewardenepura originated through the Maligakanda
Vidyodaya Pirivana, a premier indigenous educational institution started by Ven. Hikkaduwe
Sri Sumangala Thera, in year 1873.

It was elevated to University status under the provisions of Act No. 45 of 1958 and
was designated as the Vidyodaya University of Sri Lanka. It was shifted to the present
location at Gangodawila from Maligakanda and Ven. Welewitiye Soratha Nayake Thero
was appointed as the first Vice-Chancellor of the University.

By Universities Act No. 01 of 1972 all the Universities in Sri Lanka were amalgamated.
Accordingly Vidyodaya University of Sri Lanka was designated as Vidyodaya Campus.
Under the provisions of Universities Act No. 16 of 1978 it became an autonomous
University by the name of the University of Sri Jayewardanepura.

Location:
The University of Sri Jayewardenepura is located about 500 meters away from the
Wijerama Junction and 13 Km from Colombo along the Colombo - Avissawella or High
Level Road. It comes under the Nugegoda postal area, the Mirihana police area and under
the Maharagama Urban Council.

Staff:
There are 595 permanent members of the Academic staff and 12131 undergraduates.
The rest of the staff consists of nearly 1004 people.

2. UNIVERSITY ADMINISTRATION
================================================================

2.1 Introduction
Recommendations and decisions pertaining to University educational and
administrative matters are taken by the authorities established under the provisions of
the Universities Act. These authorities consist of the University Council, Senate, Faculty
Board and Boards of Studies of the Faculty of Graduate Studies.

2.2 Authorities

Council
The executive and administrative authority of the university is the Council. The
Vice-Chancellor is a member and the Chairman of the Council. The Council consists of
all Deans, representatives of the Senate and members appointed by the University Grants
Commission. The Council of the University of Sri Jayewardanepura consists of twenty
three (23) members.

Senate
The academic authority of the university is the Senate. The Vice-Chancellor is a
member and the Chairman of the Senate. The rest of the members of the Senate consists
of the Director of the Postgraduate Institute of Management, all Deans, all Heads of
Departments, all Professors, representatives of Faculties and the Librarian. The Senate of
the University of Sri Jayewardenepura consists of 147 members.

Faculty Boards
Every Faculty has a Faculty Board presided by the Dean of the Faculty. The
Faculty Board consist of Professors, Associate Professors, Senior Lecturers, Lecturers,
representatives of other academic staff, student representatives and external Faculty Board
members. The Faculty Board is the principal authority of the university. It is the Faculty
Board that makes recommendations to the Senate with regard to academic matters.

Faculty of Graduate Studies
It is the responsibility of the Faculty of Graduate Studies to organise the award of
postgraduate degrees, supervision and decisions making pertaining to them. The Faculty Board of
the Faculty of Graduate Studies will be chaired by a Dean and will consist of representatives of the
Board of Studies and representatives of the Council and other Faculties.

2.3 Posts and Services

Chancellor
As the Head of the University, the Chancellor will preside over the Convocation.

Vice-Chancellor
The Principal Executive officer and the Academic officer of the University is the
Vice-Chancellor. He is a member of the Council which is the Governing Authority and the
Senate which is the principal academic authority and the chairman of both these authorities.

Deans and Heads of Departments
Each Faculty of the University is administered by a Dean. The Dean is the academic
and administrative Head of the Faculty. The University of Sri Jayewardnepura consists of Seven
Faculties: Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences, Faculty of Applied Sciences, Faculty of
Management Studies & Commerce, Faculty of Medical Sciences, Faculty of Graduate Studies,
Faculty of Technology, and Faculty of Engineering.

Registrar
The Registrar, subject to the direction and control of the Vice-Chancellor, shall be
responsible for the general administration of the University and the disciplinary control of its
non-academic staff. He shall be responsible for the custody of the records and the property
of the University. It is the duty of the Registrar to act as the Secretary to the Council and the Senate.
The Registrar will be assisted by the Deputy Registrars, Senior Assistant Registrars
and Assistant Registrars, each responsible for different sectors of university administration
(Academic Student Affairs, Faculty administration, Examinations, Student Welfare, etc.)

Bursar and the Finance Division
The Bursar of the University shall be responsible for the administration of finances of
the University subject to the direction and control of the Registrar. The Bursar is an officer
of the University appointed by the Council. Matters relating to payments and supplies are
handled by this division. Payments by students can be made directly to the shroff-counter
of the Finance division or the People's Bank branch close to the University premises, or any
People's Bank branch within the island as directed by the University.

3. ACADEMIC ACTIVITIES AND DEGREE PROGRAMMES
================================================================

3.1 Courses and Degrees
The University of Sri Jayewardenepura conducts courses for students who have
gained admission to the university, selected based on their performance at the General
Certificate of Education (Advanced Level) examination. Students are admitted to 3 or 4
year full time degree programmes. All expenses to conduct these courses are met by the
government under the Free Education policy.

In addition, the university offers Certificate and Postgraduate Diplomas, M.A., M.Sc.,
M.Phil. and Ph.D. programmes, mostly catering to employed graduates on a part-time,
fee-levying basis. All postgraduate Degrees are conducted by the Faculty of Graduate Studies.

Faculties offering first degree programmes include: Faculty of Humanities & Social
Sciences (e.g. B.A. General/Honours in various subjects), Faculty of Management Studies
& Commerce (e.g. B.Com. Special, B.Sc. Marketing Management, B.Sc. Accounting,
B.Sc. Finance, B.Sc. Business Administration), Faculty of Applied Sciences (e.g. B.Sc.
General, B.Sc. Special Degrees in Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science, Mathematics,
Physics, Statistics), Faculty of Medical Sciences (MBBS, B.Sc. Nursing, Pharmacy, Medical
Laboratory Sciences, Human Biology), Faculty of Technology (Engineering Technology,
Biosystems Technology, Information and Communication Technology), and Faculty of
Engineering (Civil, Computer, Electrical & Electronic, Mechanical Engineering).

Postgraduate research degree courses (M.A., M.Phil., Ph.D.) are conducted under the
seven Boards of Study of the Faculty of Graduate Studies: Social Sciences, Humanities,
Management Studies & Commerce, Medical Sciences, Life Sciences, Physical Sciences,
and Multidisciplinary Studies.

3.2 Postgraduate Institute of Management
The Postgraduate Institute of Management affiliated to the University of Sri
Jayewardenepura is located at No 28, Lesley Ranagala Mawatha, Colombo 08. It is
administered by a Director and is under the supervision of the University.

4. ACADEMIC STUDENT AFFAIRS AND PUBLICATIONS DIVISION
================================================================

4.1 Registration of students for degree courses
Every student entering the university should register as a student of the university.
Subsequently, they should extend their registration at the beginning of each academic
year. Registration of students who do not report for lectures within one week of the
commencement of the academic year will be cancelled and action will be taken to admit a
similar number of students. If a student registered for a course does not attend the course
on medical grounds, they should inform the University Medical Officer of such absence.
On the recommendation of the Medical Officer, a written request may be made to the Dean
of the relevant faculty seeking a postponement of registration for the next year. The final
decision on this will be made by the Senate on the recommendation of the Faculty Board.

Along with the first registration, each student will be issued a student record book,
student identity card, and a student hand book. It is the responsibility of the student to
keep these safely. If a student misplaces their record book or identity card, they should
promptly report it to the Academic, Student Affairs & Publications Division and pay a
fine determined by the University to obtain a copy.

A student unable to complete the degree during the specified period will be granted a
further period of three consecutive years, one year at a time, by registering as a temporary
internal student. A temporary internal student will be granted permission to sit examinations
only and will not be entitled to any other benefits.

4.2 Convocation
Each year on a date decided by the Chancellor of the University, a Convocation will
be held to confer degrees to those who have passed the relevant courses. According to
university tradition, the Convocation is the most significant official function of the
University. Students can obtain their degrees without attending the Convocation (in
absentia).

4.3 Other activities
This division handles issuing letters certifying student status for scholarship
applications, submission of student requests to Senate on academic and examination
matters, conducting Senate meetings, issuing identity cards, and coordinating research
grants and allowances for academic staff.

5. STUDENT WELFARE DIVISION AND SERVICES
================================================================

5.1 Introduction
The Student Welfare Division, under the direction of a Deputy Registrar, handles
matters relating to student welfare work: hostel facilities, canteens, Mahapola scholarships,
Bursaries and other scholarships, season tickets, student unions, subject societies, student
elections, and related matters.

5.2 Hostels
43 hostels are run by the university, some within the university premises and some
in rented buildings outside. Hostel accommodation is available for 5,233 students,
approximately 42% of the total student community. In providing residential facilities, first
year and final year students are given priority, based on criteria approved by the University.
All resident students must pay the fees prescribed by the University.

5.3 Administration of Hostels
Two senior members of the academic staff are appointed as wardens (one for
male hostels, one for female hostels). Full-time and part-time sub-wardens are appointed
for day-to-day administration.

5.4 Rules and regulations for resident students
Key rules include: students must occupy assigned rooms and not change without
permission; damage to furniture/equipment incurs the cost of damage plus a 25% fine;
mattress and bed sheets must always be used; silence must be maintained; appropriate
dress is required in common areas.

Special rules include:
- Electrical appliances: strictly prohibited to use any electrical appliances other than
  those supplied by the University.
- Late attendance: students leaving the hostel must sign a register noting departure/
  return times. Female students should return before 8.30 p.m. and male students
  before 9.00 p.m. A student needing to leave for personal reasons must obtain formal
  approval from the Vice-Chancellor/Registrar/Warden/Sub-Warden.
- Liquor and Gambling: strictly prohibited to consume liquor or take drugs at the
  hostels, or come to the hostels after consuming liquor or drugs. Gambling is also
  prohibited.
- Visitors: prohibited to entertain visitors in rooms without permission of the Wardens/
  Sub-Wardens. Visitors are permitted to enter the hostel between 7.00 a.m. and 5.00 p.m.
  only.
- Checking of Rooms: Wardens can check rooms at any time at their discretion.
- Conduct of meetings: strictly prohibited to organize unions, societies, discussions or
  meetings within hostel premises without prior approval of the Vice-Chancellor.
- Medical Treatment: a vehicle pass will be issued to a sick resident student on receipt
  of a written request, arranged through the Sub-Wardens, Chief Security Officer, and
  resident Assistant Registrar.

5.5 Student Counselling Services
Members of the academic staff are appointed to advise university students on
day-to-day problems and matters relating to courses and examinations, functioning under
the Director, Student Counselling.

5.6 Mahapola Scholarships, other Scholarships and Bursaries

5.6.1 Mahapola Scholarships
Awarded and administered by the University Grants Commission and the Mahapola
Scholarship Trust Fund. Only ten (10) installments are paid per academic year. Students
who do not receive two (2) installments consecutively will have their installment stopped.
As of 2018, 5,262 students (44% of the student population) received Mahapola scholarships.

Conditions include: recipients must be engaged in full-time courses; a student who
fails to obtain an installment forfeits it (not paid retroactively); after accepting the
scholarship, a student cannot change course or receive another scholarship/bursary;
changing course, ceasing studies, or gaining employment leads to cancellation; course
postponement requires permission, granted for a maximum of one year; attendance at
lectures, tutorials and practicals is required or payments stop.

Other scholarships are also awarded by various institutions including the University
Grants Commission, Commercial Bank, Department of Public Trustee, Sri Lanka Police
Staff Welfare Society, Sri Lanka Ports Authority, and several alumni associations and
private donors.

5.6.4 Bursaries
Students who do not receive Mahapola scholarships are eligible to apply for
Bursaries, subject to governing circulars and conditions. Eligible students receive two
installments per academic year. In 2018, 2,511 students (20% of the student population)
received Bursaries.

Conditions include: false declarations lead to cancellation of registration; changes in
family income, marital status or employment must be reported in writing to the Registrar;
payment can be stopped or withheld for reasons including failure to sit examinations,
complete failure at an examination, failure to continue studies satisfactorily, or breach of
conduct; a maximum of 10 installments are paid per academic year; a student receiving a
Mahapola scholarship is not eligible for Bursaries.

5.7 Student Relief Fund
Commenced in 2007, this fund pays scholarships on a priority basis to very poor
students who satisfy requirements, up to a maximum of 10 installments per academic
year, with a value of around Rs. 1000.00 per installment.

5.8 Other Services
Additional services under the Student Welfare Division include a tailoring shop at the
Student Centre, 12 canteens providing food at concessionary rates (overseen by a Canteen
Committee appointed by the Vice-Chancellor), and milk and milk food outlets on the
university premises.

5.9 Career Guidance Unit
This unit provides advice and information to graduates on finding employment,
organizes training sessions and workshops, conducts personality and merit development
courses for undergraduates, maintains a computer center and resource center, and helps
build understanding between university students and the private sector. Contact:
careers@sjp.ac.lk.

6. STUDENT DISCIPLINE AND PUNISHMENTS
================================================================

6.1 Proctor and Deputy Proctors
Under the direction of the Vice-Chancellor, the Proctor performs duties relating to
student discipline, with powers, duties and functions found in By-Law No. 01 of 1986
(amended). Deputy Proctors are appointed from each faculty to assist the Proctor.

6.2 Marshals
Marshals are appointed to assist the Proctor and Deputy Proctors in maintaining
discipline. Their duties include intervening in breaches of peace and keeping police/
university authorities informed. Five Marshals are currently in service.

6.3 Common Guidelines on Student Discipline (UGC Circular No. 946, 10 February 2011)

Disciplinary Procedure:
1. Charges of misconduct/indiscipline against a student are first reported to the
   Vice-Chancellor (VC) or Deputy Vice-Chancellor (DVC)/Rector.
2. The VC/DVC/Rector may request further particulars, requesting a staff member to
   investigate and report within 24 hours.
3. If satisfied evidence exists, the VC/Rector appoints staff or another suitable person
   to investigate and submit conclusions/recommendations in writing within 3 working
   days.
4. In serious cases, or where a student denies guilt but evidence points to misconduct,
   the VC/Rector may impose an "Out of Bounds Declaration" pending formal inquiry,
   reporting subsequently to the Council for endorsement. This means total prohibition
   on attendance/access to the University and participation in University activities.
5. On receipt of an investigation report, the VC/Rector causes charges to be framed in
   writing and sent by registered post within 5 working days, requesting a reply within
   5 working days.
6. The VC/Rector may order withholding of examination results pending inquiry.
7. If the student pleads guilty or does not reply in time, the VC proceeds to impose
   punishment as recommended by the Board of Discipline/Senate, based on Schedule
   II (the punishment schedule), and reports to the Council.
9. If the student pleads not guilty, the VC/Rector appoints a committee of up to 3
   members to hold a formal inquiry and submit a report within 14 days.
10. A student against whom disciplinary action has been taken may appeal to the
    Council within fourteen (14) days from the date the decision was issued.

Record of Punishment:
All punishments and disciplinary actions are recorded in the student's personal file
and may be reflected in the testimonial and student record book. The Council has the
power to consider removal of punishment records, having regard to the gravity of the
offence and the good behaviour of the student, upon request.

Procedure at Formal Inquiry (Schedule I):
The charge sheet must contain specific charges, served individually. If the respondent
pleads not guilty, a disciplinary committee of up to 3 members holds a formal inquiry and
submits a report within 14 days. At the inquiry, the student has the right to defend
themselves, call witnesses, and (subject to the Disciplinary Committee's discretion in special
circumstances) question the complainant or witnesses. The Committee of Inquiry has power
to summon witnesses and any student to render assistance. After finalizing recommendations,
the Committee refers its findings to the Board of Discipline (BOD)/Senate to determine
punishment within the scope of Schedule II. The BOD consists of five members appointed
by the VC. On receipt of the BOD/Senate's determination, the VC places it before the
Council for ratification, then conveys the decision to the student by registered post.

"Out of Bounds Declaration" details: this is a total prohibition on attendance/access to
the University and participation in any University activity. It is not itself considered a
punishment, but a protective measure. A student must be given an opportunity to make
representations (in person or in writing) before the Deputy Proctor, Marshal, or Chief
Security Officer, in the presence of the Registrar or a Deputy Registrar, before such a
declaration is made — except in cases of great urgency, where the VC/Rector may impose
it immediately with written reasons given afterward. The declaration must be reviewed
every thirty calendar days, with reasons recorded if extended.

Appeals Procedure:
1. Appeals against a punishment decision are made to the VC.
2. The VC places the appeal before the next Council meeting.
3. The Council appoints a 3-member Appeals Committee, which elects its own
   Chairman; the Registrar serves as Secretary.
4. The Appeals Committee considers all appeals and submits a report to the Council
   within two weeks of receiving the appeal.
5. Members of the original Disciplinary Committee do not participate in Council
   proceedings considering the Appeals Committee's report.
6. The Council may vary the disciplinary decision based on the Appeals Committee's
   recommendation. The Council's decision on the appeal is final.
7. The VC communicates the Council's final decision to the student by registered post.

By-Law No. 01 of 1986 on Student Discipline — General:
This By-Law is operative from 12th December 1986. The Vice-Chancellor can take
appropriate measures regarding university discipline in addition to powers conferred by
the Universities Act. The Vice-Chancellor and Council are responsible for execution and
administration of this By-Law.

Board of Discipline:
Established under the By-Law, consisting of: the Deputy Vice-Chancellor, the
Proctor, the Dean of each faculty, the Director of Student Welfare, the Chief Medical
Officer, two appointed members of the Council, and the Senior Assistant Registrar (Legal).
The Registrar or nominee serves as Secretary. Appointed members hold office for three
years. The Deputy Vice-Chancellor serves as President of the Board (or a temporary
president is elected if unavailable). The quorum for a meeting is seven.

Powers and responsibilities of the Board of Discipline include: maintaining student
discipline; curtailing unrest; dispersing unauthorized meetings; investigating breaches of
discipline; advising the Council on rules and regulations; conducting inquiries and
reporting findings to the Vice-Chancellor; summoning students and staff to give evidence;
recommending temporary suspension or "out of bounds" status pending inquiry; and
appointing disciplinary committees (maximum 3 members, chaired by a Board member) to
inquire into complaints.

Part III — General Discipline: Misconduct and Breach of Discipline
Every university student should be well behaved, and this standard of good behaviour
must continue for as long as their studentship is in force. Students must engage in academic
work to the university's satisfaction; without express permission or acceptable reason, no
student should keep away from lectures or practicals for more than six weeks during an
academic year.

The following are considered misconduct and breaches of discipline: damaging
the good name of the University; obstructing educational, examination, or administrative
work; failing to produce the Record Book or Identity Card when required and establish
identity; damaging or misappropriating university property; damaging the privacy/
independence of any student, teacher, or employee, or causing them loss; forming
unauthorized unions or organizations; disrupting meetings, seminars, functions, or
processions; organizing unauthorized meetings on university premises; collecting money
or goods from students without prior written approval of the Vice-Chancellor; printing
posters/leaflets harmful to the university's good name; publishing statements harmful to
the university's reputation without authorization; bringing liquor or drugs onto university
premises, selling them, or keeping them in possession; bringing arms or explosive items
onto premises; withholding or falsifying information requested by the university;
misusing university buildings, halls, premises or equipment; and being convicted of a
criminal offence under Sri Lankan criminal procedure.

Hostel-specific misconduct includes: not adhering to rules set by university
administrators, hostel wardens, sub-wardens and student counsellors; transferring hostel
facilities to another student or changing rooms without permission; meeting outsiders or
opposite-gender students in unauthorized areas of the hostel other than designated Common
Rooms; and returning to hostels late or staying out overnight without permission (the
curfew being 8.30 p.m. for female students and 9.00 p.m. for male students, per Section 5.4).
